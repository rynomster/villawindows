            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>Do you replace or repair cracked window putty?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>We do not perform window putty repairs; this specialised work is best handled by a professional glazier or glass company.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 02 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>Do you repair broken glass or broken windows?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>We do not repair single panes of broken glass. For broken glass repairs, we recommend contacting a local glass company.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 03 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>Do you do full window restoration, including painting?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>Our work focuses on the mechanical and structural aspects of timber joinery. For paint stripping, painting, and full cosmetic refurbishment, we recommend the services of a qualified painter/decorator. While some refurbishment is included with retrofit double glazing, final painting will still be required.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 04 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>Is there a warranty on double glazing?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>Yes. Our Insulated Glass Units (IGUs) are manufactured to the relevant NZ/AUS standards by accredited manufacturers and are covered by a 10-year guarantee under the NZ Building Code.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 05 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>What are mullions or glazing bars?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>Mullions are the timber glazing bars that divide sashes into smaller panes of glass. Depending on your preference, we can retain these to preserve the heritage character of your home or remove them for a clearer, unobstructed view.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 06 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>What is "true" double glazing?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>True double glazing involves replacing single-pane glass with a newly manufactured IGU (Insulated Glass Unit) consisting of two panes of glass separated by a butyl spacer. Products that simply add a second layer of glass or acrylic over existing windows are not true double glazing and lack the same thermal benefits.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 07 -->
        <div class="faq-item stagger-item">
            <button class="faq-question">
                <h3>Do you double-glaze leadlights?</h3>
                <div class="faq-icon"></div>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
                <p>Yes, leadlights can often be 'encapsulated' into a modern IGU to provide thermal benefits while keeping the original decorative feature. Please get in touch for more specific information.</p>
            </div>
        </div>

        <!-- FAQ Item 08 -->
